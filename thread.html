I'm in the market for a new electric car and want to know what's coming in 2025. I've been following the Tesla Model 3 refresh, but I'm also interested in what other manufacturers are bringing to the table. What are you most excited about? Any hidden gems I should be aware of?

My budget is around $50,000 and I'm looking for something with at least 300 miles of range. I do a lot of highway driving, so good highway efficiency is important.

Replies
TeslaLover 1 day ago

The new Tesla Model 3 refresh (codenamed Highland) looks really promising. They've improved the suspension for a smoother ride, added ventilated seats, and the interior looks more premium. Range is expected to be about 10% better too.

The base model should start around $42,000 and get about 330 miles of range. If you can stretch your budget, the Performance version is rumored to hit 0-60 in under 3 seconds.

EVExpert 1 day ago

Don't overlook the Hyundai Ioniq 6. It's got great range (over 300 miles), super fast charging, and a really unique design. The 2025 model is expected to get some battery improvements that should push the range even further.

They're also adding a new "Eco" mode that optimizes efficiency for highway driving. I test drove one last week and was getting about 4.2 miles per kWh at 70 mph, which is excellent for an EV.

HyundaiFan 20 hours ago

I own an Ioniq 5 and can confirm Hyundai's EVs are excellent. The 800V architecture means you can charge from 10-80% in under 18 minutes at a fast charger. The 2025 Ioniq 6 is rumored to get even faster charging.

Also worth noting: Hyundai has one of the best warranty packages in the industry - 10 years/100,000 miles on the battery.
